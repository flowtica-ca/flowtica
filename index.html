Case Studies & Demos

Examples of AI workflows in practice

Flowtica works best when AI is embedded in your operations stack, not bolted on as a stand-alone chatbot. The examples below show representative patterns we build for document- and process-heavy teams, without exposing sensitive implementation details. Each example links to a dedicated page with a diagram and a practical walkthrough of the workflow architecture behind it.

Example · Property Management

Private operations assistant for multi-building portfolios

A private AI assistant for property management and condo/strata teams that answers questions about buildings, residents, and procedures from one place. Responses are grounded in your leases, manuals, bylaws, and internal documentation, with citations, so staff can verify the source quickly. The workflow connects to your existing systems so answers stay aligned with your current data.

  • Who it helps: multi-building operators where knowledge is spread across leases, SOPs, vendor docs, shared drives, and ticketing tools.
  • What changes: coordinators and managers get consistent, policy-aligned answers in seconds, plus links to the source documents and an audit-friendly record of what was referenced.
  • Typical outcomes: fewer escalations, faster response times for tenants and owners, and clearer documentation for decisions across the portfolio.

We’re onboarding 1–2 additional property management teams for a focused AI workflow MVP at founding-partner pricing (50% off) in exchange for structured feedback and a non-sensitive case study.

Example · Service Businesses

AI reception & intake for clinics and trades

An AI reception workflow that answers common questions, captures structured intake details, and routes requests so fewer calls and messages are lost when staff are busy. It can support phone, web chat, or both, and hands off cleanly to your team when a human response is needed.

  • Who it helps: clinics, trades, appointment-based services, and multi-location operators juggling phones, email, and chat.
  • What changes: every inquiry becomes a clear, structured request (who, what, where, urgency, preferred time) instead of a vague voicemail or partial message.
  • Typical outcomes: higher lead capture, faster follow-ups, and a simpler way to prioritize callbacks across locations and channels.
View detailed example

This workflow can start small, one location or one channel, then expand as you gain confidence in the handoffs, guardrails, and team process around it.

Example · Internal Operations

Policy & HR knowledge copilot

An internal assistant that helps employees self-serve answers to policy and HR questions while keeping responses aligned with approved documentation. Answers are grounded in your current handbooks and policies, with citations so people can verify the exact source quickly.

  • Who it helps: organisations with handbooks, policy docs, and internal FAQs spread across shared drives and wikis.
  • What changes: employees get accurate, up-to-date answers based on official sources rather than stale chat history or email threads.
  • Typical outcomes: fewer repetitive questions, faster onboarding, and more HR/ops time spent on higher-leverage work.
View detailed example

This pattern is reusable across HR, IT, compliance, and operations, each with scoped access, while running within the same private workflow environment.

Across all three examples, the principle is the same: a private workflow layer connects to your systems, keeps documents and data current, and lets an AI model reason over that context with access control, guardrails, and logging. Tools can change; data ownership and architecture stay in your favour.
